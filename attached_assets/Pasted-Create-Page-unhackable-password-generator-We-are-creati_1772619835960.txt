Title:

What Makes a Password Practically Unhackable?

Explain clearly.

Important concept:

A password becomes practically unhackable when the number of possible combinations becomes so large that brute-force attacks are unrealistic.

Introduce entropy concept.

Example explanation:

A randomly generated 16-character password using uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols typically contains around 105 bits of entropy.

This represents approximately:

2^105 possible combinations

Even extremely powerful GPU clusters attempting billions of guesses per second would require longer than the age of the universe to search the entire space.

This is why high-entropy random passwords are considered computationally infeasible to crack.
